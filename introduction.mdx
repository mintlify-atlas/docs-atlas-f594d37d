## What is Privacy Preserving SfM?

Privacy Preserving Structure-from-Motion (PP-SfM) is a modified version of the [COLMAP](https://github.com/colmap/colmap) pipeline developed by the [Computer Vision and Geometry Group](http://www.cvg.ethz.ch) at ETH Zurich. It enables 3D scene reconstruction while protecting the privacy of image content.

Instead of sending raw keypoint positions to a processing server — which could reveal sensitive visual content — PP-SfM replaces keypoints with **line features**. These lines encode the geometric information needed for reconstruction without exposing exact pixel locations of dynamic objects such as people, vehicles, or other identifiable content.

The approach was presented at the **European Conference on Computer Vision (ECCV) 2020**.

## How it works

The privacy-preserving approach transforms standard SIFT keypoints into **line features** before sharing data with a reconstruction server:

1. **Feature extraction** — SIFT keypoints are extracted locally on device
2. **Line conversion** — Each keypoint is converted to a line passing through the keypoint position (the exact position is hidden, but the line encodes direction constraints)
3. **Gravity alignment** — Lines parallel to the gravity direction are flagged as "aligned" and provide stronger geometric constraints
4. **Reconstruction** — The server reconstructs the 3D scene from matched lines, without ever seeing the original keypoint positions

## Research context

The project page and example datasets are available at the [CVG ETH Zurich research page](http://www.cvg.ethz.ch/research/privacy-preserving-sfm).

A follow-up version that works **without known camera calibrations** was presented at CVPR 2021. That code will be released in this repository when ready.
